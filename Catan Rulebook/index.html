Why This Game

I chose Catan because it is the quintessential "gateway game" that introduced me to the world of modern board gaming. I first discovered it during a rainy weekend at a friend's house about five years ago, and I have been hooked ever since. What makes it special is the perfect balance between strategy and social interaction through trading. I would recommend this game to anyone who enjoys competition but prefers a social atmosphere over a strictly silent, "brain-burning" experience. It is ideal for families and friend groups because the rules are simple to learn, yet every game feels different due to the modular board. If you enjoy negotiating and building empires, this is definitely the game for you.

Overview

Catan is a game about settling a newly discovered island. You play as a settler attempting to build holdings through trading and production.

The goal is to be the first player to reach 10 Victory Points on your turn.

Victory_Points >= 10
What You Need

The standard game comes with a variety of cardboard and wooden components:

  • 19 Terrain Hexes (Forest, Hills, Pasture, Fields, Mountains, and Desert)
  • 9 Harbor Pieces and 18 Number Tokens
  • 95 Resource Cards (Brick, Lumber, Wool, Grain, and Ore)
  • Wooden Settlements, Cities, and Roads in 4 colors
  • 2 Dice and 1 Robber figure
Setup

Follow these steps to prepare the island for settlement:

1. Create the frame using the sea pieces. 2. Place the terrain hexes inside the frame randomly. 3. Place the number tokens on the hexes in alphabetical order, skipping the desert.

const setupOrder = ['A', 'B', 'C', 'D', '...'];

Finally, players take turns placing two settlements and two roads on the board. You receive your starting resources based on your second settlement.

How to Play

A turn consists of three main phases in a specific order:

First, roll the dice. If the total matches a number on a tile, every player with a settlement adjacent to that tile gathers resources.

if (diceRoll == tileNumber) { gainResource(); }

Second, you may trade resources with other players or the bank. Third, you may build roads, settlements, or buy Development Cards.

Scoring and Winning

Points are earned in several ways throughout the game:

  • Settlements: 1 Point each
  • Cities: 2 Points each
  • Longest Road: 2 Points (requires at least 5 continuous road segments)
  • Largest Army: 2 Points (requires at least 3 Knight cards)
  • Victory Point Development Cards: 1 Point each

You can only win on your own turn. If you reach 10 points on someone else's turn, you must wait until your turn to claim victory.

Common Mistakes and Tips

Avoid these common pitfalls to improve your game:

The Distance Rule: You cannot place a settlement if it is only one "jump" away from another settlement. There must be at least two empty corners between them.

Tips for Success:

  • Prioritize Brick and Lumber early in the game to build roads and expand.
  • Don't hold more than 7 cards! If a 7 is rolled, you lose half your resources.
  • Try to settle on different numbers (e.g., a 6, 8, and 5) to ensure you get resources more often.
cardsInHand <= 7; // Safety threshold
FAQ and Edge Cases

Q: Can I build a road and a settlement at the same time?
A: Yes, as long as you have the resources and obey the distance rule.

Q: What happens if the bank runs out of a resource?
A: If there isn't enough of a resource for everyone who earned it, no one gets that specific resource for that roll.

Q: Can I trade away a resource I just received?
A: Yes! You can trade at any point during your trade phase.

allowTrade = true;
